Plastic Pathways
Lagrangian Particle Tracking Simulator

18,000 virtual particles were placed at the mouths of the world's most polluting rivers, then tracked for 90 days through observed ocean currents and wind patterns. This map shows where they ended up.

The simulation runs on real ocean current data from GLORYS12 and wind data from ERA5. OceanParcels drives the particle tracking, with a 2% windage correction to account for how wind pushes floating plastic

What You're Actually Looking At

Particle tracks show where each piece of virtual plastic drifted. Use the timeline to watch them grow day by day.

Beaching hotspots show where plastic accumulates on coastlines. Darker hexagons mean higher probability.

River sources are the 18 highest-polluting rivers globally (Lebreton et al. 2017). Asian rivers contribute 86% of oceanic plastic.

Validation

Simulated trajectories were compared against NOAA Global Drifter Program observations. Separation distance under 100 km at 30 days for top-performing pairs, consistent with the ~8 km resolution of GLORYS12.

Methodology
Technical Pipeline
01 - DATA ACQUISITION

Ocean surface currents from GLORYS12 reanalysis at 1/12° daily resolution. 10m wind components from ERA5, resampled to daily. Coverage: Jan 2020 – Apr 2021.

02 - PROCESSING

GLORYS12 NetCDF converted to Zarr with Dask for chunked access. ERA5 spatially subsetted, temporally resampled, longitude converted from 0–360° to ±180°.

03 - SIMULATION

OceanParcels v3.1.4, RK4 advection, 1-hour timestep. Custom windage kernel: 2% of 10m wind with latitude-dependent m-to-deg conversion (111,320 cos(φ) for lon, 110,540 for lat). Out-of-bounds particles deleted via StatusCode kernel.

04 - BEACHING DETECTION

Final positions within 0.25° (~25 km) of GSHHG coastline classified as beached. Aggregated into H3 hex cells (res 5, ~252 km²), normalized by total seeded.

05 - INFRASTRUCTURE

*Most of this infrastructure stack is overkill, I was just trying to get some hands on experience with it.* Tegola tile server containerized, deployed to minikube via Terraform with HPA. Static layers as PMTiles. Frontend: MapLibre GL JS.

06 - VALIDATION

10 GDP drifters from Jan 2020 compared via separation distance (Liu & Weisberg 2011). Sub-100 km at 30 days for best pairs. All drifters showed correct bulk transport.

Improvemnts to be made

land mask was applied,but buffers seemed to have worked improperly, so particles can drift through coastlines and landmasses, visible in some track lines. Beaching detection uses a simple proximity buffer at the final timestep rather than continuous coastline interaction. The 90-day runtime misses longer gyre accumulation patterns. Windage is a flat 2% rather than a distribution reflecting variable debris buoyancy. Particle seeding is uniform across rivers rather than weighted by actual plastic discharge estimates. A longer simulation with a land-ocean mask, continuous beaching kernel, and discharge-weighted seeding would improve it all.
